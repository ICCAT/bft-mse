Overview

Atlantic bluefin tuna has complex dynamics involving two stocks (eastern and western) with long-distance movements and mixed-stock fisheries. MSE for Atlantic bluefin tuna including stock mixing between eastern and western bluefin tuna stocks has been under development since at least 2014. After the substantial efforts by both the SCRS and the Commission, a Management Procedure (MP) was adopted in 2022 (Rec. 22-09) and the TAC was set in 2023-2025 for both stocks using this MP (East: Rec. 22-08 and West: Rec. 22-10). The Commission also adopted the Exceptional Circumstances (EC) protocol in 2023 (Rec. 23-07, Annex 4), that included the evaluation of the EC every year by the SCRS. ECs have not been invoked since 2023. The SCRS scheduled the application of the Management Procedure (MP) in 2025 to set Total Allowable Catches (TACs) for the period after 2026. In addition, the SCRS plans to conduct an assessment, as well as review the Operating Models (OMs) and the MP, after 2026.

History

Detailed histories of BFT MSE activities can be found in the SCRS Reports and the Documents/Activity Reports of the ICCAT Atlantic-Wide Research Programme for Bluefin Tuna (GBYP). The BFT MSE Process started in 2013 at the kick-off meeting in Gloucester (Anon., 2013). While Operating Models were under development, the SCRS started communicating with the Panel 2 in 2019 for operational management objectives (Res. 18-03). Since adopting the Operating Models (OMs) in 2021 (Anon., 2021), the SCRS focused on developing Candidate Management Procedures (MPs). It also engaged frequently with the Commission to support the final adoption of the MP. This interaction took place through intersessional meetings and a series of "Ambassadors webinars," which were used to present the MSE results to a broader community of stakeholders and decision-makers. The Commission also adopted the Exceptional Circumstances protocol in 2023 (Rec. 23-07 Annex 4). These developments represent the culmination of a decade-long, iterative process to establish a scientifically rigorous and operationally practical management framework for Atlantic bluefin tuna.

Operating Model Development

The SCRS developed a new unique population dynamics model for Atlantic BFT OMs to consider the mixing of the two biological populations using otolith microchemistry, genetic research data, and electronic tagging data.. This challenge took many years (since approximately 2015) up until the OMs were adopted in 2021. This was due in part to the SCRS having also had to work on the stock assessments for both East and West BFT stocks in 2017, 2020, and 2021. The adoption of these Operating Models in 2021 marked a major milestone in addressing stock mixing complexities and was achieved despite the parallel demands of completing multiple stock assessments during the same period.

Management Procedure

The Commission adopted the BR Management Procedure in 2022 (Rec.22-09, Annex 2)

Butterworth Rademeyer BR

The BR MP is empirical, based on inputs related to abundance indices which are first standardized for magnitude, then aggregated by way of a weighted average of 10 indices available for the East and the West areas, and finally smoothed over years to reduce observation error variability effects. TACs are then set based on the concept of taking a fixed proportion of the abundance present, as indicated by these aggregated and smoothed abundance indices.

Exceptional Circumstances

The Commission adopted the Exceptional Circumstances Protocol in 2023 (Rec.23-07 Annex 4).

The SCRS have evaluated annually, and there have been no Exceptional Circumstances since 2023

Principles of ECs

a: When there is evidence that the stock and/or fishery dynamics are in states not previously considered to be plausible in the context of the management strategy evaluation (MSE);

b: When there is evidence that the data required to apply the management procedure (MP) are not available or sufficient, or are no longer appropriate; and/or,

c: When there is evidence that total catch for either the West area or the East area is above the total allowable catch (TAC) for the respective area set using the MP.
